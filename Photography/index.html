Photography is the art and science of capturing light with a camera to create images. It uses a lens to focus light onto a sensitive medium, like film or a digital sensor. Originating in the 19th century, it combines creativity and technique for expression, documentation, and communication across various genres.

About Founder

Ansel Adams, born February 20, 1902, in San Francisco, was an American photographer famed for his black-and-white landscapes. Known for Yosemite National Park images, he co-developed the Zone System, enhancing photographic precision. His work, blending art and environmentalism, earned him lasting acclaim until his death in 1984.
